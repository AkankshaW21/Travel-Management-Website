Thoseghar Waterfall

History

Thoseghar Waterfall is a scenic spot located near the small village of Thoseghar, 20 km from Satara city, at the edge of the Konkan region, in Western India. It is a popular tourist destination, especially during the monsoon season, when the waterfalls are at their most spectacular.

The history of Thoseghar Waterfall is not well-documented, but it is believed to be a very ancient waterfall. The name "Thoseghar" is derived from the Sanskrit words "tusa" (water) and "gad" (fort). According to legend, the waterfall is named after the Hindu gods Rama and Lakshmana, who are said to have stayed near the waterfall during their exile.

The first recorded mention of Thoseghar Waterfall is in a British gazetteer from the 19th century. The gazetteer describes the waterfall as being "one of the most beautiful and picturesque waterfalls in the Western Ghats".

In the early 20th century, Thoseghar Waterfall became a popular tourist destination for British and Indian visitors alike. The British built a road to the waterfall, and a number of hotels and guesthouses were opened in the nearby village of Thoseghar.

After India's independence in 1947, Thoseghar Waterfall continued to be a popular tourist destination. The Indian government developed the area around the waterfall, and a number of new amenities were added, such as a picnic area and a viewing platform.


Main Features

  • Multiple waterfalls Thoseghar Waterfall is a series of waterfalls, some of them 15 to 20 metres and one of approx 200 metres in height. The main waterfall is a three-tiered fall that cascades down a rocky cliff into a pool below.
  • Lush green surroundings Thoseghar Waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests and hills. The area is home to a variety of plants and wildlife, including birds, monkeys, and butterflies.
  • Calm and quiet atmosphere The area around Thoseghar Waterfall is calm and quiet, making it a perfect place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty.
  • Picnic area and viewing platform There is a picnic area and a newly constructed platform at Thoseghar Waterfall, which gives a good view of the waterfall.
  • Religious significance There are a number of Hindu temples located near Thoseghar Waterfall, and many pilgrims visit the area every year.
  • Thoseghar Waterfall is also known for its clean water and its proximity to other popular tourist destinations, such as Satara city, Mahabaleshwar, and Panchgani.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Thoseghar Waterfall is during the monsoon season, which lasts from June to November.

During this time, the waterfall is at its fullest and most powerful, and the surrounding area is lush and green.

On weekends,It is also best to avoid visiting during the morning and evening hours, as these are the times when the waterfall is most likely to be in the shade.

You can also visit the waterfall during the winter season (December to February)

Tips Before Visiting

  • Thoseghar Waterfall is a beautiful and scenic spot that is worth a visit. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the waterfall, the lush green surroundings, and the calm and quiet atmosphere.
  • Visitors should wear comfortable shoes and clothing, as they will need to walk down a steep path to reach the waterfall.
  • It is important to be careful while swimming in the pool at the bottom of the waterfall, as the rocks can be slippery.

Other Attractions

Vasota Fort

Vasota Fort is a hill fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India. It is situated on the banks of the Shivsagar Lake, surrounded by dense forests and valleys of the Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary.

Overview

  • Altitude: 1171 m
  • Difficulty level: Medium
  • Trekking time: 2-3 hours
  • What to see: Temple, water reservoir, bastions, ruins of the fort
  • Best time to visit: October to May

Kaas Pathar

Kaas Pathar, also known as the Kaas Plateau, is a seasonal plateau of flowers located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India.

It is situated at an altitude of 1200 meters and is approximately 10 square kilometers in area.

Some best Features

  • The abundance of wildflowers
  • The variety of birds
  • The stunning scenery

Sajjangad Fort

Sajjangad Fort is a fortress located near the city of Satara, India. It is the final resting place of Sant Ramdas, a 17th century saint who is revered by many people in the state of Maharashtra.

Overview

  • Height: 3,350 feet
  • Best time to visit: October to March
  • Sajjangad Fort is also a great place to learn about the history and culture of Maharashtra
