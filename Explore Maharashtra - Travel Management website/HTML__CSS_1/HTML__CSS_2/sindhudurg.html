History

Sindhudurg is the most remarkable of Shivaji's coastal citadels. The fort which occupies an islet in the Arabian Sea, just off the coast of Maharashtra. The fortress lies on Kurte Island, half a km away from the Malvan Port in Sindhudurg District. It lies 130 km north of Goa. A protected monument, the name of this fort is given to Sindhudurg district. The fort offers breathtaking views of the sea. Sindhudurg fort was constructed by Shri Chhatrapati Shivaji when all his attempts to take the island fort of Janjira proved futile. The construction was done under the supervision of Hiroji Indulkar. It is recorded that 3000 workers and 100’s of skilled artists were employed for three years to build Sindhudurg. Shivaji had invited 100 Portuguese experts from Goa for the construction of the fort. Its fortifications extend for more than 3 km with the fortified walls, 29 ft high and 12 ft thick. The walls are partly damaged by the sea on the west. More than 50 round towers, spaced at regular intervals, have slit holes and rectangular openings for cannon. The walls of the fort are guarded by 52 bastions and some have secret exits that lead out of the fort. The fort is entered through a single Gate near the north east corner of the island which cannot be located from anywhere because it is concealed by curving outworks. On the top, there is a guardroom with arched windows. The design of the gate is unique and has a single opening bridged by a lintel set in an arched recess. A handprint and a footprint is embedded on a slab believed to be those of Shivaji himself, are seen on the inner face of the tower close to the entrance.

The interior of Sindhudurg is mostly empty, except for rubble walls, small step wells and coconut groves. A path leading west from the Gate arrives at the Shivarajeshwar Temple. It was built in 1695, by Chatrapati Shivaji’s younger son Rajaram in memory of his father. The temples is of particular interest, since it enshrines a much venerated sculpted portrait of Shivaji. The idol is shown in a seated posture, without a beard , probably only one of its’ kind in the country. The shrine is approached through a hall with triple vaulted aisles. The path continuous to the Mahadeva Temple. Originally was a well, it houses a small linga placed in one of the surrounding niches. It has a secret passage towards Shivpindi side. Nearby is Bhavani Temple. The fort also lies a unique coconut tree which has two branches. Also, there are three sweet wells named as Dudh Bav (Milk Well), Dahi Bav (Curd Well) & Sakhar Bav (Sugar well), which are the only souce of drinking water in the fort.



Major Features

Sindhudurg Fort, located in the state of Maharashtra, India, is a historic sea fort built during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It is known for its architectural significance and strategic location along the Arabian Sea coast. Some of the major features of Sindhudurg Fort include: 1)Architecture: Sindhudurg Fort is known for its impressive and robust architecture. It was constructed using laterite stone, which is a locally available rock known for its durability. The fort's walls are massive and designed to withstand the force of the sea. 2)Sea Bastions: The fort has strategically positioned bastions and watchtowers along its walls, offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea. These bastions were used for defense and surveillance against approaching ships and enemies. 3)Entrance: The fort's main entrance, known as the Dilli Darwaza (Delhi Gate), is a well-fortified and imposing gateway. It has spikes at the top to prevent elephant attacks and a concealed passage for escape during emergencies.

4)Moat: Sindhudurg Fort is surrounded by a moat filled with seawater. This moat was designed to act as a natural barrier and to prevent direct access to the fort. 5)Temples: Within the fort, there are several temples dedicated to various deities, including Lord Shiva and Goddess Bhavani. These temples are examples of ancient Maratha architecture and serve as places of worship. 6)Freshwater Wells: Despite its location in the sea, the fort has numerous freshwater wells that were carefully designed to provide a continuous supply of freshwater to the inhabitants. 7)Underground Tunnels: It is believed that Sindhudurg Fort has underground tunnels that were used for escape and to transport supplies during times of siege. 8)Historical Significance: The fort is historically significant as it was built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in the 17th century to protect the Maratha kingdom's coastline from foreign invasions.

9)Tourist Destination: Today, Sindhudurg Fort is a popular tourist destination. Visitors can explore its historical structures, enjoy scenic views, and learn about its rich history through guided tours. 10)Accessibility: The fort is accessible by boat from the Malvan coast, making the journey to the fort itself an exciting part of the experience for tourists.

Weather/Climate

Summer

Summer in Sindhudurg Fort is generally warm and can be somewhat humid. Daytime temperatures during this season typically range from 30°C to 35°C (86°F to 95°F) but can occasionally go higher. Nights are relatively cooler, with temperatures dropping to around 20°C to 25°C (68°F to 77°F). It's advisable to wear light and breathable clothing during the summer months and stay hydrated.

Monsoon

The monsoon season in Sindhudurg Fort is characterized by heavy rainfall and high humidity. The southwest monsoon arrives in June and continues through September, bringing substantial rainfall to the region. Rainfall can be intense, and the landscape becomes lush and green during this period. The humidity levels are quite high, making it feel humid and sticky. The sea can be rough during the monsoon, which may limit water-related activities.

Winter

Winter is the most pleasant time to visit Sindhudurg Fort. Daytime temperatures range from around 20°C to 30°C (68°F to 86°F), providing comfortable weather for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Nights can be cooler, with temperatures dropping to around 15°C to 20°C (59°F to 68°F). This is the best time for tourists to explore the fort and enjoy the scenic beauty of the region without the discomfort of extreme heat or heavy rain.
