Pavan Kada Waterfall

History

Pavankada Waterfall is a relatively new waterfall, having been created in the early 2000s during the construction of the Kharghar Hills development project. The waterfall was created by diverting the waters of a nearby stream into a man-made waterfall.

The waterfall is named after Pavankada Hill, which is located nearby. Pavankada Hill is a sacred hill for Hindus, and it is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Hanuman.

The Pavankada Waterfall quickly became a popular tourist destination, especially during the monsoon season when the waterfall is at its most spectacular. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and offers visitors a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in the beauty of nature.

In recent years, the Pavankada Waterfall has also become a popular spot for trekking and rappelling. The waterfall is surrounded by a number of trekking trails, which offer stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The waterfall is also a popular spot for rappelling, as the rock face is ideal for the activity.

Pavankada Waterfall is a popular tourist destination, especially during the monsoon season.


Main Features

  • Height: The waterfall is approximately 100 meters high.
  • Natural pool: The waterfall drops into a natural pool at the base, which is a popular spot for swimming.
  • Surrounding area: The waterfall is located in the Kharghar Hills, which are known for their scenic beauty and biodiversity.
  • Accessibility: The waterfall is relatively easy to reach, either by taxi or auto-rickshaw from Kharghar railway station or bus stand, or by a short trek from Kharghar.
  • Popularity Pavankada waterfall is a popular tourist destination, especially during the winter months when the weather is pleasant.

    • The water quality of the waterfall is generally good, although it is important to note that the waterfall can be closed to tourists during the monsoon season due to safety concerns.


Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Pavankada waterfall is during the winter months (November to February) when the weather is pleasant.

The waterfall is closed to tourists during the monsoon season (June to September) due to safety concerns.

During the winter months, the weather in Navi Mumbai is generally mild and sunny. This makes it ideal weather for visiting the waterfall and enjoying the surrounding area.

Tips Before Visiting

  • Avoid visiting the waterfall during the monsoon season, as the rocks can be slippery and dangerous.

  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing, as you will need to walk over slippery rocks to reach the waterfall.

  • Be careful of the monkeys that live in the area.

  • Do not visit in mansoon season ,for safety concerns.

Location

Kharghar, suburb of Navi Mumbai, Maharashtra

Nearby places you must visit

Marine Drive

Marine Drive is a 3.6-kilometer-long Promenade along the Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Road in Mumbai, India. It is a popular tourist destination, known for its scenic views of the Arabian Sea and the city skyline

Interesting Facts

  • It was constructed in the 1920s by Pallonji Mistry, a Parsi businessman.
  • The drive is named after the Prince of Wales, who visited Mumbai in 1921.
  • The drive is also known as the Queen's Necklace.
  • Marine Drive is a popular spot for filming Bollywood movies.

Gateway Of India

The Gateway of India is a basalt arch monument built in the early 20th century in Bombay, India. The monument is located at the tip of the Apollo Bunder waterfront promenade, overlooking the Arabian Sea.

Some Facts

  • The monument was designed by George Wittet.
  • The monument cost approximately 21 lakh rupees to build.
  • The Gateway of India is made of basalt stone, which is a type of volcanic rock.
  • The monument is decorated with sculptures and carvings of Hindu and Muslim motifs.

Elephanta Caves

The Elephanta Caves are a collection of rock-cut cave temples predominantly dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva. They are located on Elephanta Island, or Gharapuri, in Mumbai

Best Features

  • The Elephanta Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site located on Elephanta Island
  • The caves are a collection of rock-cut temples and sculptures dating back to the 5th and 6th centuries AD.
  • They are dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva .
  • The Caves also feature a number of other notable sculptures and architectural features

Haji Ali Dargah

Haji Ali Dargah is a mosque and dargah (tomb) located on an islet off the coast of Worli in Mumbai, India. It is one of the most iconic landmarks in Mumbai and is visited by millions of people each year.

Some Facts

  • The dargah is open to visitors of all faiths.
  • Visitors are required to dress modestly when visiting the dargah.
  • The dargah is a popular place for weddings and other celebrations.
  • The dargah is also a popular spot for filming movies and TV shows.
