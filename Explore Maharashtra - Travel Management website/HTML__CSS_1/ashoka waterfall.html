Ashoka Waterfall

History

Ashoka Waterfall, also known as Vihigaon Waterfall, is a popular tourist destination located in Vihigaon village, Thane district, Maharashtra, India.

It is a monsoon-fed waterfall, and the water cascades down from a height of about 120 feet into a pool.

The waterfall is also believed to have a religious significance for Hindus. There is a small temple located near the waterfall, where visitors come to pray and seek blessings. It is believed that the water in the waterfall is sacred and has the power to cleanse the soul.

The exact history of Ashoka Waterfall is unknown, but it is believed to be a popular spot for centuries. The waterfall is located in the Sahyadri mountain range, which has been inhabited by humans for thousands of years. It is possible that the waterfall was used by ancient civilizations for bathing, drinking, and irrigation

  • Height:Ashoka Waterfall is a monsoon-fed waterfall that cascades down from a height of about 120 feet into a poo .
  • Accessibillity :The waterfall is easily accessible by road. Visitors can take a bus or taxi from Thane to Vihigaon village. From there, it is a short walk to the waterfall.
  • Tourist facilities:The Government of Maharashtra has developed several tourist facilities at the site, such as a parking lot, a viewing platform, and a garden.
  • Cultural significance: Waterfall is also believed to have a religious significance for Hindus. There is a small temple located near the waterfall, where visitors come to pray and seek blessings.
  • Conservation efforts: The Government of Maharashtra has taken several steps to conserve Ashoka Waterfall and its surrounding area. The waterfall has been declared a protected area, and plastic is banned within the premises.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Ashoka Waterfall is during the monsoon season, from June to September.

During this time, the waterfall is at its fullest and the surrounding area is lush green.

The winter months (October to March) are also a good time to visit.

Tips Before Visiting Waterfall

  • Be aware of the weather forecast and take precautions to stay safe, especially during the monsoon season.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing, as the ground can be slippery near the waterfall.
  • Be respectful of the environment and dispose of your trash properly.

Nearby places you must visit

Vasai beach

Vasai Beach is a popular beach located in the Vasai-Virar region of Maharashtra, India. It is a long, sandy beach with a gentle slope. The beach is also known for its beautiful sunsets and its proximity to several historical and religious sites.

Things to do

  • Swim, sunbathe, and play in the sand
  • Watch the sunset
  • Visit the Vasai Fort
  • Go for a walk or bike ride along the beach
  • Visit the Vasai Virar Jain Temple

Tansa Wildlife Sanctuary:

The Tansa Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Thane district of Maharashtra, India. It was established in 1970 and covers an area of 320 square kilometers.

Highlights

  • Wildlife:This Sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife
  • Birds: The Tansa Wildlife Sanctuary is a birdwatcher's paradise, with over 250 species of birds found in the sanctuary
  • Tansa Dam
  • Nature trails
  • Boat safaris

Vaitarna Dam

Vaitarna Dam, is a gravity dam on the Vaitarna River in the Palghar . It is one of the main sources of water supply for the Mumbai metropolitan area.

Specifications

  • Dam type: Gravity dam
  • River: Vaitarna River
  • height: 74.5 meters (244 ft)
  • length: 714 meters (2,343 ft)
  • Reservoir capacity: 2,194 million cubic meters
  • Catchment area: 160.8 square kilometers

Ganeshpuri

Ganeshpuri is a small town in the Palghar district of Maharashtra, India. It is located about 100 km from Mumbai and is known for its hot water springs and the Nityananda Ashram.

Things to do

  • Take a dip in the hot water springs.
  • Visit the Nityananda Ashram.
  • Visit the Vajreshwari Temple and the Ganesh Temple.
  • Go for a hike in the nearby hills.
  • Visit the local markets.
